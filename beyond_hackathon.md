<section class="section-card">
  <p>
    The momentum does not stop when the hackathon ends. The University of Tennessee, Knoxville—together with several of our co-organizers—hosts
    <strong>automated STEM and SPM instruments that can be fully controlled through Python-based workflows</strong>.
    Beyond the hackathon, if you would like to continue developing your ideas, explore autonomous experimentation, or run your codes on real microscopes,
    please reach out. We are delighted to collaborate on <strong>joint research projects</strong> and help you deploy your workflows on physical instruments.
  </p>
  <p>
    Our partners in automation include <strong>Rama Vasudevan</strong> and <strong>Yongtao Liu</strong> at ORNL,
    <strong>Steven R. Spurgeon</strong> at the University of Colorado Boulder, and
    <strong>Marcos Penedo García</strong> and his group at EPFL.
  </p>
</section>
